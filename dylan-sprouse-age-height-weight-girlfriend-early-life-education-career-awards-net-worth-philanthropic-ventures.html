His Early Life, Parents, Education, Ethnicity And Religion

Dylan and his brother Cole grew up in Long Beach, California, where their parents, both teachers specializing in English and drama, instilled in them a diverse heritage of German, English, Scottish, Irish, and Danish roots.

From the age of six months, they embarked on an acting journey, initially sharing the role of Patrick Kelly on the TV series “Grace Under Fire” until 1998.

Their joint appearances extended to films like “Big Daddy,” “The Astronaut’s Wife,” “I Saw Mommy Kissing Santa Claus,” and TV shows such as “Friends.”

Both Dylan and Cole attended Laurel Springs High School, an online private school, graduating as valedictorians in 2010.

Subsequently, they pursued higher education at New York University’s Gallatin School of Individualized Study, with Dylan focusing on video game design and Cole on archaeology.

They completed their studies in 2015.

Dylan has identified as a Heathen, a modern form of Germanic paganism, since the age of 15.

His Personal Life And Girlfriend

In his personal life, Dylan has been in a relationship with Hungarian model Barbara Palvin since 2018.

The couple, residing in Brooklyn for over two years, announced their engagement in June 2023, revealing a private ceremony in Hungary in July 2023.

While they don’t have children, they share their lives with a dog named Nash.

Dylan has had past relationships with celebrities such as Miley Cyrus, Kara Crane, Becky Rosso, and Dayna Frazer.

Although linked to Brenda Song, his co-star from “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody,” both deny any romantic involvement.

Dylan’s Professional Career And How If Affects Her Personal Life

Dylan’s career unfolds in three phases: his early years with Cole, a hiatus from acting, and a comeback as an adult.

During their early years, the brothers co-starred in projects like “Big Daddy,” “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody,” and “The Kings of Appletown.”

Following a hiatus after “The Suite Life on Deck,” Dylan returned to acting in 2017, expressing a desire to challenge himself creatively.

His recent works include “Dismissed,” “Carte Blanche,” “Banana Split,” “After We Collided,” “The Curse of Turandot,” and “Tyger Tyger,” showcasing his versatility beyond his Disney star image.
